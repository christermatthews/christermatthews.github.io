# Public Order Team

## Executive Summary

As the department's Public Order Team Commander, I established the Plano Police Department Public Order Team from concept through full operational capability. The initiative required organizational planning, personnel recruitment, policy development, equipment procurement, training development, operational planning, and regional coordination with neighboring agencies.

The project transformed public-order response capabilities while establishing standardized tactics, improving officer safety, enhancing interoperability, and creating a sustainable long-term training program.

---

# Situation

Plano had experienced increasing demand for organized public-order capabilities capable of responding to demonstrations, civil unrest, major public events, and large-scale incidents requiring coordinated field force operations.

Although individual officers possessed valuable experience, the department lacked a formally organized and fully equipped public-order team operating under nationally recognized doctrine.

---

# Challenge

Develop a professional public-order capability while:

* Recruiting qualified personnel

* Building executive support

* Researching best practices

* Developing policies

* Selecting equipment

* Building training curriculum

* Remaining within budget

* Maintaining operational readiness

---

# Strategic Objectives

• Establish a sustainable Public Order Team

• Standardize tactics using nationally recognized doctrine

• Improve officer safety

• Increase operational flexibility

• Strengthen regional interoperability

• Build recurring monthly training

• Develop future team leaders

---

# Planning

The project began with extensive research into nationally recognized public-order programs including National Tactical Officers Association guidance and Public Order Management Academy doctrine.

Existing regional capabilities were evaluated while identifying equipment, staffing, command structure, training frequency, deployment procedures, and operational planning requirements.

---

# Stakeholders

- Police Chief
- Executive Staff
- Patrol Division
- Special Operations
- Training Unit
- Purchasing
- Finance
- Neighboring Police Agencies
- Public Order Team Members

---

# Equipment Selection

Equipment evaluations included:

- Helmets
- Shields
- Body armor
- Protective equipment
- Less-lethal platforms
- Team equipment
- Communications
- Logistics support

Research emphasized officer safety, interoperability, long-term durability, and operational effectiveness.

---

# Training Program

A recurring monthly training program was developed emphasizing:

- Individual movement
- Squad movement
- Platoon operations
- Arrest teams
- Extraction teams
- Less-lethal integration
- Crowd management
- Mobile field force tactics
- Scenario-based exercises

---

# Regional Collaboration

Worked closely with Richardson, Allen, Frisco, and McKinney Police Departments to improve regional consistency in:

- Tactics
- Equipment
- Terminology
- Communications
- Operational planning
- Mutual aid

---

# Leadership

The project required balancing operational readiness with fiscal responsibility while developing future leaders capable of sustaining the program after initial implementation.

Leadership emphasized ownership, accountability, professionalism, continuous improvement, and realistic scenario-based training.

---

# Results

The completed Public Order Team provided Plano Police Department with:

- Dedicated Public Order capability
- Standardized doctrine
- Improved officer safety
- Regional interoperability
- Sustainable monthly training
- Operational deployment capability
- Leadership succession planning

---

# Lessons Learned

Major organizational initiatives succeed when planning, communication, stakeholder involvement, and continuous evaluation remain priorities throughout implementation.

Building culture proved equally as important as acquiring equipment.
